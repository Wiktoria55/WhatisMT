Which machine translation engine is the best for you?

In today’s world, the leading machine translation systems are based on neural networks and generative AI architectures. Tencent AI, a prominent Chinese technology company, conducted a study evaluating the quality of translations from Chinese to English. Several commercially available translators were put to the test and their outputs were compared using three key metrics used for assessing the quality of machine translations: BLEU (Bilingual Evaluation Understudy), Chrf++ (character F-score), and TER (Translation Edit Rate). Participants in the study included:

• Google Translate,

• DeepL,

• Tencent’s translator,

• ChatGPT (version 3).

According to the results, Google Translate was positioned in first place, followed by DeepL in second, Tencent’s translator in third and ChatGPT in fourth. However, it is essential to note that this research focused solely on English-Chinese language pair, making it impossible to predict what results the above-mentioned translators would receive when working with Polish-English or English-Polish language pairs. Therefore, it is advisable for everyone to explore the most popular translators available for free and select the tool that best suit your needs.

1. Google Translate - Google Translate is of the most popular MT engines, which has significantly improved over the years. Transitioning from statistical MT to neural MT in 2016, it later introduced its Transformer model, enabling more accurate translations. Google supports 133 languages and offers many features, including: text, image, website, handwriting and spoken word translation, as well as offline translation.

2. DeepL - Introduced in 2017, DeepL focuses on major global languages and currently supports 32 of them. It allows free users to translate various file formats, such as: .docx, .doc, and .pptx, with a monthly limit of 100,000 characters. Paid plans unlock additional features and file formats.

3. ChatGPT – Developed by OpenAI, ChatGPT is a chatbot that uses the GPT model to generate responses. It is also capable of translating texts as well as improving the output. Users can adjust style, length and formality of texts through prompts. ChatGPT supports over 80 languages, but file translation requires a paid plan.

4. Microsoft Translator – is a popular cloud-based machine translation service, which supports 135 languages and offers text, image, limited speech and document translation for free users. It integrates seamlessly with various Microsoft products like Edge browser, Office and Skype, facilitating translation within these applications.
