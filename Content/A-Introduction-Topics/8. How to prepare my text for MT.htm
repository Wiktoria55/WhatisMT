How to prepare your text for machine translation?

Although human translators excel at understanding context, nuances and identifying errors, machine translation often struggles with these tasks. When preparing a text for machine translation, it is very important to follow certain steps to achieve the best results. Here are some tips to help you achieve this:

1. Avoid errors

a. Make sure that the text does not contain any mistyping errors. Words with typos can be mistranslated.

Writing “Wesołych Świąt spędzonych w rodzinnym grobie” (Merry Christmas spent in your family grave) has a totally different meaning than „Wesołych świąt spędzonych w rodzinnym gronie” (Merry Christmas spent with your family).

b. Check the punctuation. Neural machine engines rely on dots and commas to guess the meaning, so it is very important to use correct punctuation.

Missing commas:

• “Help a theft!” without a comma instead of “Help, a theft!” - Should you really help a thief?

2. Remember that MT tool is not a dictionary

Keep in mind that MT engines may not always be accurate when used to translate single words. They often fail to produce correct results because of homonyms (words that have multiple meanings). For example, the word “zamek” in Polish can refer to either a castle or a zipper.

3. Provide context

Ambiguities in languages are common and context helps MT engines generate more accurate results.

To-do list:

1. Eat breakfast

2. Polish shoes

Lista rzeczy do zrobienia: (To-do list:)

1. Zjedz śniadanie (Eat breakfast)

2. Polskie buty (Shoes made in Poland)

The second point was translated incorrectly due to lack of proper context.

4. Select the source language:

Make sure that you indicate the language of the source text as language detection algorithms may be wrong. For example:

sklep, zachod, obchod - do not mean the same in Polish

Mám nápad! – “I have an idea in Czech (especially when written without special characters)

The sentence may be detected as Polish, where it means “I’m having a seizure”.

5. Choose simplicity. Try to minimize the use of “special cases” such as: idioms and slang words which may not be understood by the MT engine. Remember to check whether proper names are translated correctly.

To give some examples:

a. Idioms:

“ I really love my friends. We get on like a house of fire” – “Radzimy sobie jak w domu ognistym” which is obviously wrong

b) Slang words:

- Slang words: la vache! – Oh my god!

La vache! – il a crié en la voyant dans sa nouvelle robe.

Krowa! – krzyknął widząc ją w nowej sukience.

Le poulet m'a dit : arrêtez les arnaques, s'il vous plaît.

The chicken told me: stop the scams, please.

Le poulet is a slang word for a policeman.

6. Remember about post-editing – it is very important to check the translated text to eliminate errors. Pay attention to the above-mentioned special cases: proper names -names, surnames, streets and e-mails, verify idioms and always use a dictionary whether doubts arrive.

Proper nouns : Wiktoria Ryba - Poisson Victoria, or Victoria Fish

Poisson Victoria

Rue Kościuszki

"Fairy" - the best dishwashing liquid according to research.

„Wróżka” – według badań najlepszy płyn do mycia naczyń.

7. Avoid long sentences.

Make your text easier to understand by avoiding complex sentences. Instead, try to divide it into smaller components, which are manageable – such as phrases, sentences or paragraphs. Segmentation helps to process text effectively, improving accuracy and the consistency of the output. [IMAGE]

[...] Je pris un profond intérêt à ces peintures qui étaient suspendues non seulement sur les faces principales des murs, mais aussi dans une foule de recoins que la bizarre architecture du château rendait inévitables; si bien que j’ordonnai à Pedro de fermer les lourds volets de la chambre,– puisqu’il faisait déjà nuit,– d’allumer un grand candélabre à plusieurs branches placé près de mon chevet, et d’ouvrir tout grands les rideaux de velours noir garnis de crépines qui entouraient le lit.

[...] bardzo zainteresowałem się tymi obrazami, które wisiały nie tylko na głównych ścianach ścian, ale także w wielu zakamarkach, w których znajdowały się dziwaczne architektura zamku sprawiła, że było to nieuniknione; do tego stopnia, że kazałem Pedro zamknąć ciężkie okiennice pokoju, gdyż było już ciemno, zapalić duży kandelabr z kilkoma gałęziami, ustawiony przy moim łóżku i szeroko rozchylić zasłony z czarnego aksamitu obszyte sitkami, które otoczyła łóżko.
